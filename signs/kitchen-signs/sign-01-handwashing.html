Food Safety — Handwashing Protocol
Wash Your Hands
California Retail Food Code §113953 requires handwashing before food handling and after any contamination event.
1
WET hands and wrists with warm running water (100°F+)
2
APPLY soap — scrub vigorously for at least 20 seconds: palms · backs of hands · between fingers · under nails · wrists
3
RINSE thoroughly under clean running water
4
DRY with a single-use paper towel
5
USE the paper towel to turn off the faucet — do not touch faucet with bare hands

Wash hands at these times:

On arrival After restroom After raw seafood After touching face/hair After removing gloves After handling trash After breaks After sneezing/coughing After handling money
Failure to wash hands properly is a critical violation under California Health Code and may result in permit suspension.
