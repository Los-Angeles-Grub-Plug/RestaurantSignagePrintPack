Food Safety — Handwashing Protocol
Wash Your Hands
California Retail Food Code §113953 requires handwashing before food handling and after any contamination event.
1
WET hands and wrists with warm running water (100°F+)
2
APPLY soap — scrub vigorously for at least 20 seconds: palms · backs of hands · between fingers · under nails · wrists
3
RINSE thoroughly under clean running water
4
DRY with a single-use paper towel
5
USE the paper towel to turn off the faucet — do not touch with bare hands

Wash hands at these times:

On arrivalAfter restroomAfter raw seafoodAfter touching face/hair After removing glovesAfter handling trashAfter breaksAfter sneezing/coughingAfter handling money
Failure to wash hands properly is a critical violation under California Health Code and may result in permit suspension.

Sign 2 of 12 · Kitchen Safety Series

Food Safety — Glove Protocol
Gloves Required
California Retail Food Code §113961 — All employees handling ready-to-eat food must wear single-use gloves.

Change Gloves When:

→
Between different tasks or food types
→
Every 4 hours during continuous use
→
After touching face, hair, or body
→
After handling raw product
→
If torn, punctured, or contaminated
→
After using the restroom

Proper Glove Use:

1
Wash hands before putting on gloves — always
2
Select the correct size — too loose = contamination risk
3
Do not blow into gloves or roll them on surfaces
4
Remove by peeling from wrist — avoid touching the outside
5
Dispose immediately — gloves are single-use only
⚠ Gloves do NOT replace handwashing. Wash hands before every new pair.

Sign 8 of 12 · Kitchen Policy

Kitchen Policy
No Cell Phones
in the Kitchen

Personal cell phones are not permitted in the kitchen or food prep areas during your shift.

Cell phones harbor bacteria and create cross-contamination risks when handled alongside food.

Store your phone in your bag, locker, or designated area before entering the kitchen.
Exception: Managers may carry phones for operational use only — no social media or personal calls on the line.
Violation: Using a phone in the kitchen is a policy violation and may result in disciplinary action.

Breaks and personal calls must be taken in the designated break area only.

California Legal Requirement — AB 1953 / SB 602
California Food Handler Card Required
California SB 602 — All food employees must obtain a valid California Food Handler Card within 30 days of hire. Cards must be renewed every 3 years.

What You Need to Know:

1
Complete an accredited food handler course (online or in-person)
2
Pass the food safety exam with 70% or higher
3
Receive your card — valid for 3 years
4
Keep your original card on your person during all shifts
5
Renew before expiration — expired cards are not accepted

Approved Providers:

→
StateFoodSafety.com — Online, ~$15, available in English & Spanish
→
ServSafe.com — Online or in-person, ANSI-accredited
→
eFoodHandlers.com — Online, low cost, CA-approved
→
Any ANSI/ASTM-accredited food handler program
Employees without a valid Food Handler Card may not work food service shifts. This is a California state law requirement.
